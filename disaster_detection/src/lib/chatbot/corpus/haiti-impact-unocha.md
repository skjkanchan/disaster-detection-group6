# Hurricane Matthew in Haiti

Hurricane Matthew made landfall on Haiti's southwestern Tiburon Peninsula near Les Anglais on October 4, 2016 at approximately 11:00 UTC as a Category 4 hurricane with sustained winds of 230 km/h (145 mph). It was the most powerful storm to strike Haiti since Hurricane Cleo in 1964.

## Casualties and Displacement

- Official Haitian government death toll: 546 confirmed fatalities, with humanitarian organizations estimating the actual total at 1,000 or more.
- Approximately 175,500 people were displaced to shelters at the peak of the emergency.
- An estimated 2.1 million people were affected across the departments of Grand'Anse, Sud, Nippes, Ouest, and Nord-Ouest.
- 1.4 million people, including 592,000 children, required immediate humanitarian assistance.

## Infrastructure and Housing

- More than 200,000 homes were damaged or destroyed; entire villages in Grand'Anse and Sud were flattened by the combined effects of wind and storm surge.
- The town of Jérémie reported damage to 80% of its structures.
- Road access to the southern peninsula was severed for several days after the Petit-Goâve bridge was compromised by flooding.
- 90% of crops in the affected departments were destroyed, triggering long-running food insecurity.

## Health Emergency

A cholera outbreak followed the storm; the World Health Organization recorded over 3,400 suspected new cholera cases in the month after landfall, concentrated in Grand'Anse and Sud. International partners launched a $120 million appeal for emergency health, shelter, water, and sanitation assistance.
