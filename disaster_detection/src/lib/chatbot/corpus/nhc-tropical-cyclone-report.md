# NHC Tropical Cyclone Report: Hurricane Matthew

Matthew formed from a tropical wave that moved off the African coast on September 22, 2016 and became a tropical storm east of the Windward Islands on September 28. It reached Category 5 intensity with 1-minute sustained winds of 165 mph (270 km/h) and a minimum central pressure of 934 mb on October 1, 2016 over the central Caribbean Sea.

## Track and Landfalls

- October 4, 2016, 11:00 UTC: Landfall near Les Anglais, Haiti as a Category 4 hurricane (145 mph / 230 km/h).
- October 4, 2016, 24:00 UTC: Landfall on eastern Cuba near Juaco, Guantánamo Province as a Category 4 hurricane.
- October 6, 2016: Passed directly over Nassau, Bahamas as a Category 3 hurricane.
- October 7, 2016: Paralleled the Florida east coast, with the eye remaining offshore.
- October 8, 2016, 15:00 UTC: Landfall near McClellanville, South Carolina as a Category 1 hurricane.

## Meteorological Records

- Matthew was the southernmost Category 5 Atlantic hurricane on record (at 13.4°N).
- It was the longest-lived Category 4–5 Atlantic hurricane in the satellite era since Hurricane Ivan (2004).
- Matthew produced the highest storm surge on record at the Fernandina Beach, Florida tide gauge (5.84 ft above mean higher high water).

## Overall Damage and Deaths (All Countries)

The NHC catalogued at least 585 direct deaths across all countries, with indirect deaths bringing the total well above 700. Matthew caused an estimated $16.47 billion (2016 USD) in damage worldwide, making it the costliest Atlantic hurricane since Sandy (2012) at the time.
