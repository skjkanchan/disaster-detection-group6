# Hurricane Matthew — FEMA After-Action Overview

Hurricane Matthew was an extremely powerful, long-lived, and deadly tropical cyclone that became the first Category 5 Atlantic hurricane since Felix in 2007. Matthew caused widespread devastation across Haiti, Cuba, the Bahamas, and the southeastern United States between late September and mid-October 2016.

## Federal Declarations

The Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA) coordinated the U.S. federal response across four Major Disaster Declarations:

- DR-4283, Florida, declared October 8, 2016.
- DR-4285, Georgia, declared October 8, 2016.
- DR-4286, South Carolina, declared October 11, 2016.
- DR-4287, North Carolina, declared October 10, 2016.

Individual Assistance was authorized for affected counties, and Public Assistance categories A–G were activated for eligible applicants.

## Impact Summary in the United States

- At least 49 direct and indirect deaths were attributed to Matthew in the United States.
- More than 2.2 million residents were placed under mandatory evacuation across Florida, Georgia, South Carolina, and North Carolina.
- Peak power outages exceeded 2.2 million customers across the four states.
- Estimated U.S. economic damages totaled approximately $10 billion USD.
- FEMA obligated over $1.5 billion in federal assistance across the four declarations within the first year.

## Damage Characteristics

Damage to residential structures in the Carolinas was dominated by inland flooding from record-setting rainfall, particularly in the Lumber, Neuse, and Tar river basins. Coastal Florida and Georgia saw wind-driven roof damage, storm-surge inundation, and beach erosion. FEMA inspectors used a five-class damage scale (destroyed, major, minor, affected, inaccessible) that is broadly compatible with the xBD xView2 damage labels used in this dashboard.
