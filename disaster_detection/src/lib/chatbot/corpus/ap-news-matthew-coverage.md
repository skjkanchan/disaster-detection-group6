# AP News Archive — Hurricane Matthew

## October 4, 2016 — Haiti landfall

The Associated Press reported that Hurricane Matthew slammed into Haiti's southwestern coast at dawn, tearing off rooftops, uprooting trees, and severing the sole bridge linking the southern peninsula to the capital. Les Anglais and Port-à-Piment suffered near-total destruction of wooden and tin-roof homes. Early reports indicated dozens dead and thousands without shelter.

## October 6, 2016 — Bahamas and Florida evacuation orders

Governor Rick Scott of Florida ordered mandatory evacuations for 1.5 million coastal residents. South Carolina Governor Nikki Haley ordered evacuation of coastal counties, and Georgia Governor Nathan Deal extended a similar order east of Interstate 95. AP noted that the evacuation was the largest in the Southeast United States since Hurricane Floyd (1999).

## October 8, 2016 — U.S. landfall near McClellanville, SC

Matthew made landfall in South Carolina as a Category 1 hurricane with sustained winds of 75 mph. The storm produced 10–15 inches of rain across eastern North Carolina, overwhelming the Neuse and Tar river basins. Lumberton, Fair Bluff, and Princeville experienced catastrophic flooding in the following week. AP reported that more than 1.6 million customers were without power at the storm's peak across Florida, Georgia, and the Carolinas.

## October 9–16, 2016 — Aftermath in North Carolina

AP coverage in the week after landfall focused on river flooding and dam risk in North Carolina. At least 28 North Carolinians died in flood-related incidents, and the town of Princeville — the first town chartered by freed slaves in the United States — was inundated for a second time in 17 years. Federal disaster declarations followed.

## October 15, 2016 — Global death toll

By the middle of October, AP estimated the combined death toll across Haiti, Cuba, the Dominican Republic, the United States, Colombia, and St. Vincent at more than 1,000, making Matthew the deadliest Atlantic hurricane since Hurricane Stan in 2005.
